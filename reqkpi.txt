Executive Summary: The Autonomy Index Approach
To drive measurable automation maturity across Virgin Media O2 (NeuralMimicry), this framework aligns with TM Forum Autonomy Index (Levels 0–5). The framework assesses autonomy for each domain - RAN, Core, Transport, IP, Fixed Access, and Telco Cloud - using SDN capabilities, CI/CD maturity, and measurable KPIs. It integrates DORA metrics, AI-driven forecasting, and operational data pipelines to enable full lifecycle automation across planning, build, run, and optimise phases.

How is the Index Calculated?

The TMForum model defines several levels of autonomy, typically ranging from:
Level 0: Manual operation (no automation)
Level 1: Assisted automation (basic scripting, some tool support)
Level 2: Partial automation (automated workflows, human-in-the-loop)
Level 3: Conditional automation (systems can act autonomously under certain conditions)
Level 4: High automation (systems act autonomously, humans monitor)
Level 5: Full autonomy (end-to-end closed-loop automation, minimal human oversight)
Each domain and process is assessed against these levels, considering both the automation technology in place and the use of AI for decision-making, prediction, and optimization.
Why is the Autonomy Level Index Important for NeuralMimicry?

Benchmarking: Enables NeuralMimicry to benchmark its automation maturity against industry standards and peers.
Strategic Planning: Identifies areas where further automation or AI investment will yield the greatest operational and business impact.
Cross-Domain Consistency: Ensures that automation progress is measured consistently across Mobile, Fixed, B2B, and Cloud domains.
Continuous Improvement: Supports ongoing improvement by highlighting gaps and tracking progress over time.
